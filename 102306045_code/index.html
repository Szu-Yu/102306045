About Us

Life can be stressful enough, learning should not be.

Angol Falu is an innovative and friendly teaching program with a difference. The efficiency of our conversation-oriented method lies in the creation of an entirely native environment. Course material is compiled in a way so as to prepare you for real-life English. By polishing your English at Angol Falu, your language knowledge reaches a level that enables you to not to have any difficulties when it comes to using English in various professional situations.

Each and every single lesson should motivate and inspire a language learner to not only communicate well but leave them with a lasting positive experience. Why? Because this is how we remember and it is how we learn most effectively; having positive and memorable connections to the experience of learning. Through overcoming a variety of challenges, you can achieve lasting results. All with a rich tea flavour of Britain thrown in.

Retrospect

Volunteer's

Words

I was in Hungary for 40 days doing voluntary work. Most of time, I was in a super rural place named Bukkosd with other volunteers and natives. The weather there was astonishing, just like my short life oversea. Sometimes it was so hot. The heat was unforgettable. However, sometimes we had pouring rain. Even the last moment it was sunny and we could feel the sunburn. I treasure every single moment I had in Hungary, no matter I had bitterness and sour or happiness and joy. The summer was like a dream, a lucid dream. I thought I was going to wake up in Taiwan and knew it was anything but truth. However, it is a philosophy question. When is dreaming? When is in the reality? How do you define the truth?

Some people love this camp. I love this camp as well although there is a lot of (I mean, seriously, a lot) problems. I met many Hungarians and we are friends till now. This voluntary work provided me a good chance to see the local life and culture from inside. I was with them for 4 weeks and they were nice and lovely. Besides, befriending with volunteers is the best thing ever! I love them so much. I had a period of time in the camp because of something. They are the reason I was hanging on there. We have been close since we all agreed the manager is handful. We shared our own cultures, drank, danced, gossiped and had fun. We spent so much time together. It was like a big family. I missed the last few days most because the camp was over and it was our party time. We could do whatever we wanted. Of course, drinking, singing and dancing are inevitable. We didn’t need a nightclub or bar. All we got was ourselves and that was enough. I learned to how to enjoy and party in the camp. It was something I had never thought about. I saw another way to live in the moment. Most of the volunteers were students and some were travelers. They changed my values toward lives. I spent many years studying and following the path this society wanted us to. I did not even dare to think about other options. They showed me there was something else I could choose. Travelers accept uncertainty. Unlike me, I liked to spend time worrying about the future, planning for the future, trying to predict the future. As if figuring it out would somehow cushion the below. Moreover, my sight was so narrow and I was not brave enough to test my limits. Now, after I came back to Taiwan, I have to say I really miss that place. Although in the beginning, I felt frustrated because we were so far away from the capital and all I wanted to do was going back to my comfort zone, the downtown. I fell in love with rural place later. People there were friendly in an incredible way. The most remarkable thing was stars. They weaved into the curtain made of the night. So beautiful and peaceful. However, I still don’t want to spend too much time in farm again.

ANGOL FALU
